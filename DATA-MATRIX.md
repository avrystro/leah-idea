# Data Matrix - Exercise to Mental Health Condition Mappings

All data extracted from the JWB "Move Your Mental Health" Report (2022).

## Evidence Strength Key

- **strong** = 80%+ of studies showed significant positive results, supported by meta-analyses
- **moderate** = 60-79% positive, or strong individual studies but fewer meta-analyses
- **promising** = some positive results but limited studies or mixed quality
- **insufficient** = too few studies to draw conclusions

## Exercise Categories and Their Evidence

### Yoga
- Depression: **strong** (102/114 studies positive = 89%, 41 meta-analyses/reviews)
- General Anxiety: **strong** (76/82 positive = 93%)
- State Anxiety: **strong** (majority of 26 studies positive)
- Trait Anxiety: **strong** (majority of 19 studies positive)
- Stress: **moderate** (positive but fewer dedicated studies)
- PTSD: **moderate** (13 literature reviews, "encouraging but preliminary")
- Schizophrenia: **insufficient** (6/8 reviews showed non-significant results)
- Settings: home, gym/studio
- Preference: mindful
- Starter: "Follow a beginner yoga video for [duration]. Focus on breathing and gentle poses."
- Report note: GABA hypothesis - yoga increases GABA neurotransmitter activity, associated with improved mood. Also increases BDNF and decreases cortisol.

### Tai Chi & Qigong
- Depression: **strong** (33/34 studies positive = 97%)
- General Anxiety: **strong** (14/14 positive = 100%)
- Stress: **strong** (all studies positive)
- Mood: **moderate** (positive results)
- PTSD: **promising** (limited studies, positive)
- Substance Abuse: **promising** (limited studies, positive)
- Schizophrenia: **moderate** (positive for some symptoms)
- Settings: home, outdoor, gym/studio
- Preference: mindful
- Starter: "Try a beginner Tai Chi or Qigong video for [duration]. Slow, flowing movements with deep breathing."
- Report note: One study showed Qigong increased telomerase activity (cellular longevity). Also improves cardiovascular markers.

### Cardiovascular/Aerobic Exercise
- Depression: **strong** (138/161 positive = 86%, multiple meta-analyses, "medium to large effect sizes")
- General Anxiety: **strong** (59/75 positive = 79%, but report calls it "moderate but reliable")
- Schizophrenia: **moderate** (significant for negative symptoms, some for positive symptoms)
- General Mental Health: **strong** (37/40 meta-analyses positive)
- Mood: **strong** (significant improvements)
- PTSD: **moderate** (positive results in reviews)
- SMI: **moderate** (Stubbs 2018 meta-review supports it)
- Settings: home, outdoor, gym
- Preference: energetic
- Starter: "Get your heart rate up for [duration] - brisk walking, jogging, dancing, or any movement that gets you breathing harder."
- Report note: Recommended dose for depression: moderate-intensity, 3-4 days/week, 30-45 min, for at least 9 weeks. Effects comparable to antidepressants or psychotherapy.

### Walking
- Depression: **strong** (34/39 positive = 87%)
- General Anxiety: **strong** (all studies positive)
- Mood: **strong** (all studies positive)
- Stress: **strong** (all studies positive)
- General Mental Health: **strong** (7/8 positive)
- Schizophrenia: **moderate** (positive results)
- Eating Disorders: **moderate** (positive results)
- SMI: **moderate** (positive results)
- Settings: outdoor, home (treadmill)
- Preference: mixed
- Starter: "Go for a [duration] walk. Pick a pace that feels brisk but comfortable - you should be able to talk but feel slightly out of breath."
- Report note: Dose-response relationship - more walking = fewer symptoms. Even low levels (few hours/week) reduce risk of developing depression. Green exercise (outdoors) adds extra benefit.

### Running & Jogging
- General Anxiety: **strong** (5/5 positive = 100%)
- Depression: **moderate** (positive but fewer studies)
- Stress: **promising** (positive results)
- Panic Disorder: **promising** (positive results)
- Substance Abuse: **promising** (positive results)
- Eating Disorders: **promising** (mixed - positive for females, not males)
- Settings: outdoor, gym (treadmill)
- Preference: energetic
- Starter: "Start with a [duration] jog at an easy pace. Walk when you need to, then jog again. No pressure to go fast."
- Report note: Optimal frequency, duration, and intensity remain unknown. Only 10 studies total.

### Strength/Resistance Training
- Depression: **strong** (majority of 27 studies positive, especially in older adults)
- General Anxiety: **moderate** (positive results in 9 studies)
- General Mental Health: **moderate** (7 studies, positive)
- State Anxiety: **moderate** (7 studies, positive)
- Schizophrenia: **promising** (strength training for large muscle groups improved psychopathology)
- Settings: home, gym
- Preference: energetic
- Starter: "Do a [duration] bodyweight strength routine - squats, push-ups, lunges. Start with what feels manageable."
- Report note: High-intensity more effective for depression (61% showed 50%+ symptom reduction vs 29% low-intensity). But start low to avoid acute anxiety increase. Progressive increase over time is ideal.

### Cycling
- Anxiety: **strong** (13/14 positive = 93%)
- Depression: **strong** (all studies positive)
- Mood: **strong** (positive)
- Schizophrenia: **moderate** (positive, including outdoor cycling)
- Settings: outdoor, gym (stationary)
- Preference: energetic
- Starter: "Ride a bike for [duration] - outdoors or stationary. Keep a steady pace that gets your heart rate up."
- Report note: Virtual reality nature imagery during indoor cycling significantly increased stress relief vs art imagery. Only 19 studies total but 100% positive for depression.

### Dance & Movement Therapy
- Depression: **moderate** (14/16 positive = 88%)
- Schizophrenia: **promising** (5/5 showed at least one positive outcome)
- Anxiety: **promising** (limited studies, positive in reviews)
- Stress: **promising** (positive in reviews)
- Settings: home, gym/studio
- Preference: mixed
- Starter: "Put on music you love and dance for [duration]. No rules, just move. Or try a beginner dance class."
- Report note: Dance movement therapy in adolescents reduced psychological distress and increased serotonin. Quality of evidence is low - needs more RCTs. But 26 studies, mostly positive.

### Pilates
- Depression: **strong** (12/13 positive = 92%)
- General Anxiety: **moderate** (positive results)
- Trait Anxiety: **moderate** (positive)
- Schizophrenia: **promising** (6 weeks improved psychiatric symptoms)
- Settings: home, gym/studio
- Preference: mindful
- Starter: "Follow a beginner Pilates video for [duration]. Focus on core, posture, and controlled breathing."
- Report note: One 60-min session/week over several weeks showed improvements. Large reductions in depression and anxiety per meta-analysis. Feasible home-based without equipment.

### Swimming/Aquatic
- Depression: **strong** (9/13 positive)
- Anxiety: **strong** (8/13 positive)
- General Mental Health: **moderate** (positive)
- Settings: gym (pool)
- Preference: mixed
- Starter: "Swim laps or do water exercises for [duration] at a comfortable pace."
- Report note: Low-impact. Moderate to high-intensity aquatic exercise sufficient for mental health changes. May lower oxidative stress indicators. One review suggested swimming could replace antidepressant medication with consistent doses.

### Sports (Team/Organized)
- Depression: **strong** (10/12 positive = 83%)
- General Mental Health: **strong** (positive across reviews)
- Self-esteem: **moderate** (positive, especially adolescents)
- Settings: outdoor, gym
- Preference: energetic
- Starter: "Join a recreational sports league or pickup game - basketball, soccer, tennis, whatever sounds fun. Aim for [duration] of play."
- Report note: Team sports, cycling, and aerobic/gym exercise are top 3 forms associated with 20%+ fewer "poor mental health" days per month (Chekroud 2018). Social support + endorphins + self-esteem mediate the effect.

### High-Intensity Exercise (HIIT)
- Depression: **strong** (14/19 positive = 74%)
- Anxiety: **moderate** (positive, superior to low-intensity)
- General Mental Health: **moderate** (83% of meta-analyses positive)
- Mood: **moderate** (positive)
- Stress: **moderate** (positive)
- Settings: home, gym
- Preference: energetic
- Starter: "Do [duration] of interval training - 30 seconds hard effort, 60 seconds rest. Repeat. Bodyweight exercises work great."
- Report note: Just 15 minutes improved positive affect and reduced distress in patients with depression and schizophrenia. Increases BDNF (brain plasticity). But can increase anxiety immediately post-exercise in some people. Not recommended for sedentary beginners.

### Flexibility & Stretching
- State Anxiety: **moderate** (2/2 positive)
- PTSD: **promising** (positive)
- General Mental Health: **promising** (positive)
- Depression: **mixed** (2/3 null, 1 positive)
- Stress: **insufficient** (null results)
- Settings: home
- Preference: mindful
- Starter: "Do a gentle [duration] stretching routine. Focus on breathing deeply as you hold each stretch."
- Report note: Only 8 studies. Those showing positive results included breathing regimens, posture/body awareness, and were easy-to-follow home-based exercises. Decreased salivary cortisol.

### Martial Arts
- Anxiety: **mixed** (2 null, 3 positive)
- Depression: **promising** (positive in 3 studies)
- Stress: **promising** (positive)
- Settings: gym/studio
- Preference: energetic
- Starter: "Try a beginner martial arts class for [duration] - karate, jiu-jitsu, kickboxing, whatever interests you."
- Report note: Only 5 studies. Slower-tempo styles emphasizing consciousness and breath showed strongest mental health effects. Police officers with martial arts experience performed better under anxiety.

### Balance & Functional Exercise
- Depression: **promising** (positive results)
- General Anxiety: **promising** (positive)
- General Mental Health: **promising** (positive)
- Settings: home
- Preference: mindful
- Starter: "Practice balance exercises for [duration] - single-leg stands, gentle weight shifts, heel-to-toe walking."
- Report note: Only 5 studies. Home-based DVD-delivered interventions showed reductions in depression and anxiety in older adults. Being able to trust one's balance more may improve mental health through self-efficacy.

### Games & Exergames
- Depression: **promising** (positive across 6 studies)
- Anxiety: **promising** (positive)
- Schizophrenia: **promising** (positive)
- Settings: home
- Preference: mixed
- Starter: "Play an active video game for [duration] - dance games, VR sports, anything that gets you moving."
- Report note: Only 6 studies but all positive. Exergames produced higher positive emotions than traditional exercise in subclinical depression. Virtual supervision as effective as in-person.

## Key Report Statistics for Results Page

- 1,623 studies reviewed (1990-2022)
- 89% of all studies found significant positive relationships between exercise and mental health
- 89% of 720 randomized controlled trials found positive results
- Top recommendation: 3-5 sessions of 30-45 min moderate-vigorous exercise per week
- Team sports, cycling, aerobic/gym = 20%+ fewer poor mental health days/month
- Combining aerobic + resistance training shows stronger benefits than either alone
- Mindfulness-based activities (yoga, tai chi) deliver more mental health benefits than walking
- Even low levels of physical activity (walking a few hours/week) reduce risk of developing depression
